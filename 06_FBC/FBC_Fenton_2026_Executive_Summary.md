**2026 VISION — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

*First Baptist Church of Fenton*

**Where We Are**

FBC Fenton gathers roughly 300 people each Sunday. The preaching calendar is walking through Acts under the banner It Was Birthed by the Spirit, Not Built by Bricks. Staff, deacons, and ministry teams are in place. The next move is alignment — one plan, one calendar, one pathway.

**Where We Are Going**

A healthy, biblically governed local church with a clear discipleship pathway, a plurality of elders, strong men's and women's ministries, and a congregation that carries the mission rather than watching it.

**The Five-Step Pathway**

- Belong — guests become family through hospitality and follow-up.

- Grow — members are formed through Word, sacraments, and small groups.

- Serve — every member is trained and placed in ministry.

- Lead — faithful servants are raised up as shepherds.

- Multiply — groups, leaders, and mission expand beyond us.

**Six Strategic Pillars (2026–2028)**

- Discipleship Pathway — clear, repeatable, measurable.

- Worship & Guest Follow-Up — strengthen the front door.

- Small Groups & Mentoring — community that forms disciples.

- Membership & Volunteer Systems — onboard and deploy.

- Elder Plurality & Admin Team — share the shepherding load.

- Mission Here and Beyond — Fenton, Thailand, India.

**2026 Focus — Foundation Year**

- Finalize the church constitution.

- Form and empower an administration team.

- Launch the discipleship pathway churchwide.

- See the largest Easter attendance in church history.

- Execute the Thailand trip (May/June).

- Align every ministry team to one year-long plan.

**What We Are Measuring**

- Sunday attendance and guest retention.

- Small group participation and multiplication.

- New members added and volunteers deployed.

- Leaders identified, trained, and affirmed.

**What This Costs**

Alignment costs comfort. A clear pathway means we stop doing things that do not form disciples, even things we love. A plurality of elders means shared authority. A congregation that owns the mission means pastors stop carrying what was never theirs to carry. This is the cost. It is worth it.
