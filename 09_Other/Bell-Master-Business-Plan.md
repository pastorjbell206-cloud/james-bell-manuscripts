**JAMES C. BELL**

**MASTER BUSINESS & PLATFORM PLAN**

*Five-Year Strategy for Writing, Speaking, Substack, Books & Social Media*

Built April 2026 \| Horizon: 2026-2030 \| Target: \$250K+ annual platform revenue by 2030

Three Organizations: First Baptist Church of Fenton \| LiveWell \| Pastors Connection Network

**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

This is the master business plan for the writing, speaking, and content platform of James C. Bell — pastor, author of 25 published books, founder of Pastors Connection Network, and creator of LiveWell. The plan covers a five-year horizon (2026-2030) and integrates five revenue streams: Substack, books, speaking, social media, and ministry training products.

The plan does three things at once. First, it protects pastoral vocation. The local church (FBC Fenton) is not a content farm for the platform — it is the primary calling. Every revenue stream is structured to serve that calling, not to compete with it. Second, it builds a sustainable income that allows the family to thrive without lifestyle inflation, with every additional dollar above baseline routed into long-term wealth. Third, it creates a content engine where one piece of work — a sermon, an article, a book chapter — gets repurposed into seven downstream assets across multiple platforms.

Five-year financial target: \$250,000+ in annual platform revenue by 2030, on top of \$80K-\$95K church compensation, with 70% of platform income reinvested. This produces a projected net worth of \$4.2M+ at age 67, fully tax-optimized using clergy-specific structures (housing allowance, 403(b)(9), accountable reimbursements, Roth IRAs).

**The Five Convictions Behind This Plan**

1.  Faithfulness over scale. The platform serves the message, not the other way around. If growth requires compromising voice, integrity, or pastoral commitments, growth is not worth it.

2.  One voice, three organizations. FBC, LiveWell, and PCN share a unified theological foundation, brand identity, and content system. Different audiences, different registers, same author.

3.  Repurpose, do not republish. Every sermon becomes a Substack article, social posts, video clips, a book chapter draft, and a PCN training asset. One creation, seven outputs.

4.  Compounding beats velocity. Building an audience of 10,000 deep readers over five years is more valuable than 100,000 shallow followers in eighteen months. Slow burn over hot flash.

5.  Reinvest aggressively. Every dollar earned beyond baseline lifestyle goes to investments. Lifestyle ratchets up only when absolutely required (kids, health, sabbatical).

**10. THE CONTENT ENGINE — REPURPOSE SYSTEM**

**The Core Principle**

One creation produces seven outputs. A single sermon manuscript becomes: (1) a Substack essay, (2) 8-12 social posts, (3) a YouTube full video, (4) 3-5 short clips, (5) a podcast episode (when launched), (6) a book chapter draft, (7) a PCN training asset. This is the only sustainable model for one person producing across multiple channels at the volume required.

**Workflow: From Sermon to Seven Outputs**

|  |  |  |  |
|----|----|----|----|
| **Day** | **Task** | **Output** | **Where it goes** |
| Sunday | Preach sermon | Audio + manuscript | FBC YouTube |
| Monday AM | Edit manuscript to Substack format | Essay 2,500-4,000 words | Substack queue (Wed) |
| Monday PM | Pull 10-15 quote/hook tweets | Social pack | X scheduled (next 7 days) |
| Tuesday | Cut 3-5 video clips (60s-3min) | Reels/Shorts | IG, TikTok, YouTube Shorts |
| Tuesday | Create quote graphics (3-5) | Square graphics | IG feed, FB |
| Wednesday | Record audio version of essay | Podcast-ready | Substack audio + future podcast |
| Friday | Identify book chapter contribution | Tagged file | Book project folder |
| Monthly | Compile pastor-specific takeaways | PCN asset | PCN newsletter / training |

**Burnout Prevention Protocol**

- Sabbath: One full day per week zero work, zero email, zero social — non-negotiable

- Annual sabbatical: 2 weeks minimum every year; 4 weeks every 4 years

- Weekly hour cap: 60 hours total across all three orgs

- Monthly check-in with PCN brother — accountability for actual hours

- Therapy / spiritual direction: Quarterly minimum

- Family time blocks: Tuesday/Thursday evenings + every Saturday

- Date night: Weekly minimum with Susanna

**FBC-Platform Tension Management**

This is the single biggest risk to the plan. Mitigations:

- Annual deacon meeting on outside ministry time — full transparency on hours, income, expectations

- Document all sermon-derived content as ministry work (it is); separate platform-original content

- Tithe income from platform to FBC at 10% minimum (currently 'tithing in other ways')

- Platform schedule built around FBC calendar — never compete on Sundays, sermon prep, or staff meetings

- Annual review: Confirm FBC and platform are mutually reinforcing; if not, recalibrate
