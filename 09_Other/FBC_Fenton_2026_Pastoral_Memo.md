**TO THE STAFF AND DEACONS**

*From Pastor James Bell*

*April 2026*

Team,

What you have in your hands is not a program. It is a plan — ours, not mine. I am asking you to read it, pray over it, push back on it where you need to, and then help me carry it.

**Why Now**

We are a church that preaches Acts while living in Acts 1 — waiting, gathered, unsure what the Spirit will do next. The people God has given us deserve more than a full calendar. They deserve a pathway. A clear way to belong, grow, serve, lead, and multiply. That is what 2026 is for.

**What I Am Asking of You**

- Own the pathway. Every ministry team ties its work to Belong, Grow, Serve, Lead, or Multiply. If it does not fit, we ask whether it should continue.

- Carry the calendar. The year-long plan is not a suggestion. Your ministry's milestones live on it.

- Shepherd the transition. Admin team, elder plurality, constitution — these changes will surface anxiety. Name it. Pastor it. Do not dismiss it.

- Protect the soul of the work. Systems serve faithfulness; they do not replace it. If our plans start feeling corporate, we stop and pray.

**What I Am Asking of Myself**

- Preach Acts with clarity, week after week, without apology.

- Stay in the rooms where shepherding actually happens.

- Finish the constitution, form the admin team, and release what is not mine to carry.

- Guard Susanna, the boys, and the home — this plan is not worth it if that goes.

**A Word on Pace**

We have laid out a lot. I do not intend to do all of it in January. We are building a three-year arc. 2026 is Foundation. 2027 is Momentum. 2028 is Multiplication. You will hear me say it often: healthy before big. Bigger fixes nothing. Healthier changes everything.

**A Word on Unity**

We will not agree on every detail. I do not need us to. I need us to trust each other enough to disagree well, decide together, and walk out of the room on the same page. That is what a plurality of shepherds looks like.

**What I Am Grateful For**

You. The way you have loved this church. The way you have loved my family. The way you keep showing up when it costs you something. There is no one else I would rather do this with.

Your brother and pastor,

James
