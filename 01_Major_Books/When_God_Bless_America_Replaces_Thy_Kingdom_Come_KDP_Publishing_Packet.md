2\. Amazon Description

When God Bless America Replaces Thy Kingdom Come is a prophetic, pastoral, and theologically serious examination of what happens when the church begins to treat patriotism as practical salvation.

This is not a book against love of country. It is a book against misplaced worship.

In a time when many Christians speak of politics with more urgency than prayer, defend leaders with more passion than holiness, and respond to elections with more emotion than the resurrection, James Bell asks a hard but necessary question: What do you actually believe preserves the church?

Drawing from Scripture, church history, and pastoral experience, Bell argues that the greatest threat to American Christianity is not persecution but seduction. The church is not mainly being crushed from the outside. It is being quietly discipled by fear, power, outrage, and the false promise that political strength can do what only Christ can do.

Using the biblical language of spiritual adultery, this book exposes how believers can keep the language of faith while relocating their deepest trust somewhere else. We still sing. We still gather. We still say Jesus is Lord. But in practice, many have begun to look to parties, candidates, courts, cultural influence, and national power as the true guardians of the faith.

Across chapters on Hosea, the kings of Israel, the temptation of Jesus, the early church’s political witness, civil religion, Christian nationalism, Constantine, Germany, generational hypocrisy, and competing theologies of power, Bell shows how easily the church can confuse the survival of a nation with the survival of the Kingdom of God.

This is not a book about who to vote for. It is a book about who you worship.

With years of pastoral experience and a passion for theological clarity, Bell writes at the intersection of Scripture, church history, discipleship, and public witness.
